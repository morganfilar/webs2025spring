Budget Travel

1. Travel Off-Season

Flights and hotels are much cheaper when you avoid peak tourist times. Traveling during the shoulder seasons (spring and fall) can save you hundreds and still offer beautiful weather!

2. Be Flexible with Flights

Use tools like Google Flights or Hopper to track price drops and explore cheaper days to fly. Tuesday and Wednesday flights are usually less expensive.

3. Stay in Budget-Friendly Accommodations

Hostels, Airbnbs, and even couchsurfing can be great money-savers. Plus, you’ll often meet locals or other travelers with great tips!

4. Spend on Experiences, Not Souvenirs

Memories last longer than things. Prioritize experiences like food tours, nature hikes, and local events instead of shopping for stuff you might never use.

5. Eat Like a Local

Skip the overpriced tourist spots. Ask locals where they eat or visit markets and street food stalls—it’s cheaper and way more authentic!

6. Use Public Transportation

Instead of renting cars or using ride-shares constantly, try buses, trains, or subways. They’re usually efficient and give you a local perspective.
